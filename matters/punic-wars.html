A Small Summary Of The Punic Wars

 The Punic Wars were a series of three wars fought by
Rome and Carthage between 264 BCE and 146 BCE. The wars were named after the Latin word for Phoenician, “Punicus,” as Carthage
was a Phoenician colony. The wars were fought over control of trade routes and territories in the western Mediterranean.
The First Punic War (264-241 BCE) was fought over control of Sicily. The Second Punic War (218-201 BCE) was fought
over control of Spain. The Third Punic War (149-146 BCE) was fought over Carthage’s growing power in Africa. Rome emerged victorious
in all three wars, and Carthage was destroyed in 146 BCE.

    The second punic wars


    Certainly! The Second Punic War was fought
    between Carthage and Rome between 218 and 201 BCE . The war involved confrontations in Spain, Italy, Sicily, Sardinia, and North Africa

    the Alps with his army to invade Italy . However, the Romans had their own great general Scipio Africanus, who ultimately defeated Hannibal
    at the Battle of Zama in 202 BCE .

    What was Hannibal's strategy?
